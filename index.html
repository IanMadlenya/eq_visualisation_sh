Welcome to the OptimalBI earthquake data visualisation app. The visualisation represents the most recent 100 earthquakes above Magnitude 2 detected and reported by Geonet.

Each of the bubbles on the chart represents an earthquake; you can hover your mouse over a bubble to see its location on the map, or touch it if youâ€™re looking at this on your phone. You can also hover over one of the quakes represented by a circle on the map to see which bubble on the chart it corresponds to.

The bubbles are plotted on the chart on a timescale running left to right; the bubble closest to the right is the most recent earthquake. The distance of the bubble from the top of the chart indicates the depth of the earthquake. The size and colour of the bubbles represent their magnitude; the larger and brighter the bubble, the stronger the earthquake.
